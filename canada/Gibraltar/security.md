# Safety and security

## Safety and security

### Crime

Although the crime rate is low, petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, occurs. Thieves occasionally target unattended vehicles.

People walking at night between La Línea de la Concepción, in Spain, and Gibraltar have been attacked and robbed.

* Keep your personal belongings, including your passport and other travel documents, in a secure place
* Do not leave valuables in your vehicle and always lock your doors
* Park your vehicle in a secure area, especially at night

### Demonstrations

Demonstrations may occur. Even peaceful demonstrations can turn violent. They can also lead to disruptions to traffic and public transportation.

* Avoid areas where demonstrations and large gatherings are taking place
* Follow the instructions of local authorities
* Monitor local media for information on ongoing demonstrations

[Mass gatherings (large-scale events)](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/mass-gatherings)

### Terrorism

While there have been no recent terrorist attacks in Gibraltar, they can still happen. There is a threat of terrorism in Europe. Terrorists have carried out attacks in several European cities and further attacks are likely.

Targets could include:

* government buildings, including police stations
* schools, including universities
* places of worship
* airports and other transportation hubs and networks
* public areas such as tourist attractions, restaurants, shopping centres, markets, hotels and other sites frequented by foreigners
* cultural venues

Always be aware of your surroundings when in public places.

Be particularly vigilant during:

* sporting events
* religious holidays
* public celebrations
* major political events, such as elections

Terrorists may use such occasions to mount attacks.

The Government of the United Kingdom maintains a public alert system on terrorism in Gibraltar. The current threat level is 2, “moderate,” on a scale of 5. Changes to the national threat level are communicated online and through local media.

[Threat levels](http://www.police.gi/information/counter-terrorism/threat-levels) – Royal Gibraltar Police

### Water activities

Coastal waters can be dangerous. Always obey warning flags at beaches.

In marine areas, coral, jellyfish and other ocean life found along reefs can poison, sting or cause infection if touched or stepped on.

In the fall and winter months, be cautious when walking along beaches close to the water’s edge, as waves can be unpredictable in size and may come onto shore further than expected.

* Consult residents and tour operators for information on possible hazards, the presence of dangerous species and safe swimming areas
* Do not visit beaches or coastal areas during periods of severe weather warnings
* Look out for signs of cliff erosion and falling rocks
* Do not dive into unknown waters, as hidden rocks or shallow depths can cause serious injury or death
* Exercise caution and follow the advice of the local authorities

[Adventure travel health and safety](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/adventure-travellers)

### Wildlife

Large groups of Barbary Macaques, known locally as “rock apes,” are present in Gibraltar.

Macaques can get aggressive and rapidly overwhelm travellers in their search for food. They can also steal your belongings.

* Always maintain a safe distance when observing macaques
* Do not feed them, as it is illegal and you may be heavily fined

[Ape Management](https://www.gibraltar.gov.gi/environment/ape-management) – Government of Gibraltar

### Roads

Roads are tight and narrow. Always drive defensively.
