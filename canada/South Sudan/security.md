# Safety and security

## Safety and security

### Security situation

The security situation remains volatile and unpredictable across the country. Political tensions are high, and clashes between rival factions continue to occur.

Although conditions in Juba appear calm, the situation can deteriorate quickly, and outbreaks of violence remain possible.

Violent armed clashes between security forces, opposition groups and armed militias continue in several states. The risk of further escalation cannot be ruled out.

Inter-communal violence and cattle rustling also occur frequently throughout the country.

Border areas are particularly dangerous due to the presence of armed militias, criminal groups, and ongoing conflicts in neighboring countries.

* Avoid demonstrations and large gatherings
* Monitor local and international media to stay informed of the latest developments
* Keep a supply of water, food, medication and other essential supplies
* Keep your communication devices charged and secure backup power sources

### Political situation

Political tensions have intensified since the 2018 peace agreement was renewed, extending the transitional period until February 2027. In March 2025, rivalry between the main political forces escalated to armed clashes. The run-up to the general elections scheduled for December 2026 could further exacerbate tensions.

### Crime

#### Petty crime

Petty crime, such as bag snatching, occurs regularly and mainly in Juba.

During your stay in South Sudan:

* ensure that your personal belongings, including your passport and other travel documents, are secure at all times
* avoid showing signs of affluence or wearing expensive jewellery
* avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuables

#### Violent crime

There is widespread violent crime throughout South Sudan. Incidents include:

* kidnappings
* armed robbery
* carjackings
* compound invasions

The persistent economic crisis has led to a significant increase in gun-related crimes.

There are frequent armed invasions of the compounds housing international non-governmental organizations.

Incidences are especially high in Juba, which has seen regular outbreaks of violence and lawlessness.

Crime usually rises in the run-up to Christmas, New Year and Easter seasons. Foreigners have been victims of many crimes, including rape and robbery.

Law enforcement personnel have limited capacity to deter crime.

### Kidnappings

There is a risk of kidnapping, particularly in isolated areas. Both nationals and foreigners are targeted by criminal groups and militias for ransom.

* exercise caution at all times
* get professional security advice
* change your travel patterns regularly

### Restrictions on movement

Roadblocks are common and are frequently erected after dark.

Increasingly frequent fuel shortages may impede your ability to move freely at short notice and could lead to unexpected shortages of basic foodstuffs.

* Have your identity and vehicle documents readily available
* Follow the instructions of local authorities
* Take adequate precautions

### Demonstrations

Demonstrations may occur. Even peaceful demonstrations can turn violent at any time. They can also lead to disruptions to traffic and public transportation.

* Avoid areas where demonstrations and large gatherings are taking place
* Follow the instructions of local authorities
* Monitor local media for information on ongoing demonstrations

[Mass gatherings (large-scale events)](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/mass-gatherings)

### Communications

Telecommunications are unreliable.

There are no fixed telephone lines. Mobile devices don't have full functionality.

There is no international postal service.

### Road safety

Travel outside of Juba is especially risky due to frequent armed attacks, kidnappings, and the presence of landmines. Many checkpoints, both official and criminal, are set up throughout the country, where vehicles are regularly stopped and searched, sometimes leading to violent incidents.

Road conditions are excessively poor, increasing the risk of roadside ambushes targeting travellers. Many roads are unpaved and not maintained. A four-wheel-drive vehicle is required, except in some urban areas such as Juba and Malakal, especially during the rainy season from March to November.

You should avoid all overland travel across South Sudan, even if you are with an experienced guide. If you still decide to travel outside of Juba, make sure you have the following essential equipment and supplies:

* a shovel
* traction ramps for sand
* a GPS device
* reliable means of communication
* spare fuel and tires
* water and food supplies

### 2SLGBTQI+ persons

South Sudanese law prohibits sexual acts between individuals of the same sex.

2SLGBTQI+ persons are frequently subjected to physical and verbal harassment by both the local population and authorities. 2SLGBTQI+ persons should carefully consider the risks of travelling to South Sudan.

[Travel and your sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression and sex characteristics](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/lgbt-travel)

### Public transportation

A few taxis are available in urban centres but are generally old and uncomfortable.

Public transportation is limited outside of major urban areas.

Most buses are irregularly scheduled, poorly maintained and, driven very unsafely. Fatal accidents involving buses are common.

### Air travel

We don't make assessments on the compliance of foreign domestic airlines with international safety standards.

[Information about foreign domestic airlines](https://travel.gc.ca/air/in-flight-safety#other)
