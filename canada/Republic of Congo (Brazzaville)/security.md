# Safety and security

## Safety and security

### Pool Department

The security situation in the Pool Department has improved since the signing of a ceasefire agreement in 2017 between the government and the main rebel group active in the region.

Despite this improvement, tensions and violence, including civil unrest, can occur at any time, particularly during election periods.

Traffic is now possible on the main roads that were previously targeted by rebel groups. However, criminal and armed groups are still present in the area.

If you are traveling in the Pool Department:

* stay on Route nationale 1 (RN1)
* avoid travelling after dark
* always keep doors and windows closed and locked
* avoid stopping in isolated areas
* do not stop on the road to offer help to others
* be wary of strangers offering to help you

[More advice on travelling to remote areas](#remote)

### Border area with the Central African Republic

Criminal gangs and armed militias are active along the border in the Central African Republic (CAR). Possible cross-border incursions pose risks on the Congolese side.

### Border area with the Democratic Republic of the Congo

Be cautious if you’re travelling to the northern border area of the Congo along the Ubangi River, due to the prevailing insecurity in the neighbouring Democratic Republic of the Congo.

### Crime

Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, can occur, particularly in isolated neighbourhoods of major cities. Armed robberies can also occur in these neighbourhoods, especially at night. Thefts also occur on the beaches of Pointe-Noire.

During your stay:

* keep your personal belongings, including your passport and other travel documents, in a secure place
* avoid showing signs of affluence or wearing expensive jewellery
* do not carry large sums of cash or valuables unnecessarily
* avoid walking alone after dark
* avoid isolated neighbourhoods and beaches
* walk towards the traffic so you can see the vehicles approaching
* do not resist an attack

Break-ins and burglaries at residences can occur in Brazzaville and Pointe‑Noire. Foreigners’ homes have been targeted.

* Stay in accommodations with adequate security measures
* Keep doors and windows closed and locked at all times
* Keep valuables out of sight and out of reach

### Demonstrations

Demonstrations occur, especially in Brazzaville, Pointe-Noire, and the Pool Department. Even peaceful demonstrations can turn violent at any time. In the past, certain demonstrations have led to violent incidents that have resulted in deaths and injuries. They can also lead to significant disruptions to traffic and public transportation.

* Avoid areas where demonstrations and large gatherings are taking place
* Follow the instructions of local authorities
* Monitor local media for information on ongoing demonstrations

[Mass gatherings (large-scale events)](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/mass-gatherings)

### Roads

#### Road safety

There are major road safety issues. Fatal accidents are common and often involve pedestrians. Police and emergency services are extremely limited, or even non-existent, in rural areas.

#### Road conditions

Roads are generally in poor condition. Route nationale 1 and Route nationale 2 are paved, while secondary roads are not. Road conditions deteriorate significantly during the rainy seasons.

Driving can also be dangerous due to:

* the presence of many motorcycles, cyclists and pedestrians
* poorly maintained roads and potholes
* stray animals
* lack of traffic signs
* traffic jams in urban areas
* poorly maintained vehicles
* insufficient lighting and vehicles not using their headlights

#### Driving habits

Drivers don’t always respect traffic laws and speeding is common. Right of way is not given to pedestrians or other drivers. Drinking and driving is common.

If you choose to drive in the Republic of the Congo:

* always drive defensively
* always keep doors and windows closed and locked
* never leave personal belongings unattended in a vehicle, even in the trunk
* consider using a four-wheel-drive vehicle if you plan to travel outside major cities
* during the rainy seasons, confirm with local authorities if your route is passable

### Travelling in remote areas

Tourism infrastructure, including hotels, restaurants, and gas stations, is limited outside major cities.

If you are planning to travel to remote areas:

* avoid travelling after dark outside urban areas
* keep a supply of water, food, fuel, and an emergency kit on hand
* carry spare tires
* always keep your gas tank at least half full
* bring a reliable means of communication, such as a cell phone (in areas where service is reliable), a satellite phone, or a VHF radio
* keep your mobile devices charged and carry a power bank

#### Roadblocks

The army and the police set up roadblocks, which are sometimes poorly marked, throughout the country.

* Always carry your identification with you and be prepared to show it
* Follow the instructions of security forces
* Remain calm and courteous

### Power outages

Power outages occur throughout the country. Local authorities may limit electricity usage. Buildings are not usually equipped with generators.

Power outages could impact essential services, such as:

* the public water supply
* telecommunications
* banking transactions

### Telecommunications

Cell phone coverage is generally good in major cities but may be limited outside urban areas.

### Swimming

Coastal waters can be dangerous, even for experienced swimmers. Riptides are common. Several drownings occur every year. Beaches are generally unsupervised. There is no warning system to alert of unsafe conditions.

Swimming in the rapids of the Congo River and its tributaries is dangerous due to strong currents.

### Public transportation

#### Buses

Buses and minibuses are overloaded, frequently involved in accidents and prone to theft.

#### Taxis and ride-sharing services

Taxi availability is limited outside city centres.

* Use only officially marked taxis, which are green and white in Brazzaville, and blue and white in Pointe-Noire
* Never share a taxi with strangers
* Make sure the driver doesn’t pick up other passengers on the way to your destination
* Always ask the driver for identification before accepting transportation and check that it matches the name of the car registration and taxi licence
* Ask the driver to start the meter or negotiate the fare in advance
* Have small bills available for payment

Ride-sharing services are available.

* Use a trusted ride-sharing application
* Confirm the driver’s identity and licence plate before getting into the car
* Do not share your ride with another person
* Use a security feature to share your ride status with a contact

#### Trains

Trains are slow, overcrowded, poorly maintained and unsafe.

#### Ferry

A regular ferry service connects Brazzaville and Kinshasa. Accidents could occur due to overloading and poor maintenance of some vessels.

* Only use the services of a reputable company
* Make sure the appropriate safety equipment is available
* Do not board vessels that appear overloaded or unseaworthy
* You must have a valid visa to board a boat bound for the Republic of the Congo or the Democratic Republic of the Congo

### Women’s safety

Women travelling alone may be subject to some forms of harassment and verbal abuse.

[Advice for women travellers](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/advice-for-women-travellers "Advice for women travellers")

### 2SLGBTQI+ persons

Congolese law does not criminalize sexual acts between individuals of the same sex. However, homosexuality is not socially accepted and 2SLGBTQI+ persons have been subject to physical and verbal harassment by the local population and authorities.

2SLGBTQI+ travellers should avoid public displays of affection and carefully consider the risks of travelling to the Republic of the Congo.

[Travel and your sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression and sex characteristics](https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/health-safety/lgbt-travel)

### Piracy

Pirate attacks and armed robberies against ships occur in coastal waters. Travellers should take appropriate precautions.
