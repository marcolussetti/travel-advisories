# Local laws

#### Local law

You're subject to all local laws and penalties, including those that may appear harsh by Australian standards. Research local laws before travelling.

If you're arrested or jailed, the Australian Government will do what it can to help you under ourÂ [Consular Services Charter](/consular-services/consular-services-charter "Consular Services Charter"). But we can't get you out of trouble or out of jail.

#### Drugs

Penalties for drug offences are severe and include prison sentences.

**More information:**

* [Carrying or using drugs](/before-you-go/laws/drugs "Carrying or using drugs")

#### Other laws

By law, you must always carry ID with you, such as:

* a passport (must be original, copies are not accepted)
* other photo ID

You must have permission from the Ministry of Information to photograph:

* government buildings
* other infrastructure
* officials

It's illegal to export or import the local currency, Meticais.

You must have a licence to purchase or trade endangered wildlife products, such as ivory and rhino horn.

**More information:**

* [Wildlife trade](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/environment/wildlife-trade)

#### Australian laws

Some Australian criminal laws still apply when you're overseas. If you break these laws, you may face prosecution in Australia.
