## Travel to Russian Federation overview

### **Security Status**

Do Not Travel

### **Travel Alert**

Irish citizens should not travel to Russia.

Due to staffing restrictions imposed by the Russian Government, the Embassy has significantly fewer resources to assist in any crisis. Irish citizens, seeking to leave Russia at short notice, should not rely on the possibility of an organised evacuation occurring and should instead have their own plans for departure in place.

Dual Irish-Russian citizens should be aware that the Russian Government only recognises Russian citizenship for the purposes of local law enforcement and administration, including in areas of compulsory military service. The Embassy has very limited abilities to provide consular support to Irish-Russian dual citizens. The risk of arbitrary detention is high; Russian and Irish citizens have been detained and sentenced for social media posts.

Information on current departure options from Russia is below. The Embassy strongly recommends that Irish Citizens research their options for multiple possible departures, via an air route from Russia, and a land crossing into EU neighbouring states. The Embassy notes that should the situation become more volatile, air routes could be booked out very quickly or become unavailable. Irish citizens should also be aware that the EU has listed 22 Russian airlines on its [Air Safety List](https://transport.ec.europa.eu/transport-themes/eu-air-safety-list_en), as those carriers do not fulfil international safety standards. If travelling domestically in Russia, Irish citizens may want to consider alternative transportation options to flying.

Irish citizens resident in Russia, who have not already done so, are strongly recommended to register with the Embassy using the Department’s [**Citizens' Registration facility**](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/citizens-registration/). The Embassy uses the register to share important information with the Irish community.

#### **Embassy Operations**

While normal operating hours continue in both the Embassy and the Visa Office, citizens should be aware of significant delays in processing documentation, including passports. This is due both to reduced staffing in the Embassy and ongoing international post and shipping difficulties.

Citizens should also be mindful of the reduced ability of the Embassy to provide support in crisis scenarios, particularly for citizens situated far from Moscow.

If you have a query or need to make an appointment, please [**contact the Embassy**](https://www.dfa.ie/irish-embassy/russia/contact-us/).

If you have a query regarding Irish visas, please [**contact the Visa Office**](https://www.dfa.ie/irish-embassy/russia/contact-us/visa-queries/).

#### **EU Sanctions and Effects on Business**

Since the invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, the EU has introduced the most extensive restrictive measures (sanctions) in its history. These sanctions include measures targeting specific sectors, including the financial, energy, technology, defence, media and transport sectors, as well as individual measures targeting named persons and entities. EU restrictive measures are legally binding on all natural and legal persons in Ireland and the EU. EU Council Regulations have direct effect, and must therefore be complied with in the same way as domestic Irish legislation. In Ireland, it is a criminal offence to contravene EU sanctions.

All persons and entities considering doing business with Russian entities should ensure that their activities do not contravene EU sanctions, including the sanctions introduced since February 2022. Detailed information on sanctions in respect of the situation in Ukraine is available [**here**](https://www.gov.ie/en/publications/?q=&sort_by=published_date&type=press_releases&type=general_publications&type=speeches&organisation=department-of-foreign-affairs). [**The EU Sanctions Map**](https://www.sanctionsmap.eu/#/main) has information on all EU Sanctions Regimes in effect, as well as lists of the individuals and entities sanctioned under each Regime, and links to the EU legal acts for each Regime. A consolidated list of persons, groups and entities subject to EU financial sanctions is available via this [**page**](https://finance.ec.europa.eu/eu-and-world/sanctions-restrictive-measures/overview-sanctions-and-related-resources_en).

#### **Banking Restrictions in Russia**

Irish citizens in Russia should be mindful that severe limitations on banking, including the withdrawal of Visa and Mastercard services, directly affect their ability to access their money for the foreseeable future. Any citizens who have made the decision to travel to Russia should ensure they have adequate cash supplies for their stay and for emergencies, if they do not have a source of funding within Russia.

#### **Transport Routes Out of Russia**

There are severely limited or no flight routes directly to the EU. The Irish Embassy is unable to offer support with travel routes at this time.

Unfortunately, it is not possible to predict when direct flight routes to EU countries will resume.

Options also exist for overland departure from the Russian Federation through Latvia and Estonia.

Citizens intending on taking overland routes to neighbouring EU countries should check the [**travel advice**](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/advice/) and current border regulations for those countries before booking tickets and ensure they comply with entry regulations.

**General Travel Advice**

The Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade strongly recommends against all travel to Russia at this time. If you must travel to Russia, you are advised to respect the validity of your Russian visa. A visa over-stay of even a few hours can have serious consequences potentially involving a court appearance, fine, and travel ban of up to 5 years.

A system of electronic visas has been introduced by the Russian Government in Summer 2023. More information on the details of this scheme can be found [**here.**](https://electronic-visa.kdmid.ru/)

Further information on entry requirements, visa requests, or immigration issues should be directed to your local Russian Embassy.

The Embassy strongly advises against all travel to regions bordering Ukraine (Bryansk, Kursk, Belgorod, Voronezh, Rostov, and Krasnodar), due to the active conflict.

Given the volatile security situation in the North Caucasus, we strongly advise against all travel to Chechnya, Ingushetia, Dagestan and to the eastern and southern parts of Stavropol Krai bordering Chechnya and Dagestan, including the Budyonnovsky, Levkumsky, Neftekumsky, Stepnovsky and Kursky regions.

We also advise against all but essential travel to North Ossetia, Karachai-Cherkessia and Kabardino-Balkaria (including the Elbrus region). Terrorism and kidnapping are a risk in these areas.

You can contact the emergency services in the Russian Federation by dialing 112.

Specific emergency numbers are:

* Police: 102
* Fire brigade: 101
* Ambulance: 103

Irish citizens resident in Russia who have not already done so are recommended to register their details with the Embassy [**here**](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/citizens-registration/).
