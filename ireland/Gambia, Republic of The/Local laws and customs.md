## Local laws and customs

The Gambia is a predominantly Muslim society, and you should respect local traditions, customs, and laws.

Remember, the local laws apply to you as a visitor and it is your responsibility to follow them. Be sensitive to local customs, traditions and practices as your behaviour may be seen as improper, hostile or may even be illegal.

### **Ramadan**

During Ramadan, Muslims are not permitted to eat, drink, or smoke during daylight hours. You should be aware that during Ramadan fasting can cause tiredness, particularly in the late afternoon and early evening, and roads are significantly busier and subsequently more dangerous approaching sunset.

### **LGBTIQ+**

Same-sex sexual activity is illegal in The Gambia and could lead to lengthy prison sentences.

Same-sex relationships and public displays of affection are not tolerated, and same-sex marriages are not recognised. Travellers are advised to exercise caution and discretion.

Gambian law criminalises the act of men dressing as women with a five year prison sentence.

More information for LGBTIQ+ travellers is available at the following link: [**LGBTIQ+ | Travel | Department of Foreign Affairs | Ireland.ie**](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/know-before-you-go/lgbtiq/)
